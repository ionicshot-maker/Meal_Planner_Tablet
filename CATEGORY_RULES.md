# Ingredient Category Rules

These rules take priority over simple keyword matching.

The goal is **not** to categorize based on what a product contains. The goal **is** to
categorize based on how a household shops, meal plans, and uses the product.

## Meat & Poultry

**Definition:** Raw or primary meal-building protein ingredients.

**Examples:** Ground Beef, Chicken Breast, Rotisserie Chicken, Pork Chops, Turkey Breast,
Beef Roast, Steak, Pre-cooked Chicken Strips

**Do not place here:** Beef Jerky, Slim Jims, Chicken Nuggets, Frozen Chicken Patties

**Reason:** These items are generally consumed as snacks or prepared foods rather than
used as core recipe proteins.

## Eggs

**Definition:** Egg products used as eggs.

**Examples:** Organic Eggs, Large White Eggs, Cage-Free Eggs, Hard-Boiled Eggs

## Snacks

**Definition:** Foods primarily purchased and consumed as snacks.

**Examples:** Chips, Crackers, Trail Mix, Protein Bars, Granola Bars, Beef Jerky, Slim Jims

**Place here even if:** Product is meat-based.

**Reason:** Category reflects shopping behavior rather than ingredient composition.

## Frozen

**Definition:** Prepared foods primarily purchased frozen.

**Examples:** Chicken Nuggets, Frozen Pizza, Frozen Burritos, Fish Sticks, Frozen Dinners

**Do not place here:** Raw frozen chicken breast, Raw frozen vegetables

**Reason:** Frozen category represents prepared frozen convenience foods.

## Produce

**Definition:** Fresh fruits and vegetables.

**Examples:** Apples, Bananas, Lettuce, Onions, Fresh Spinach

**Do not place here:** Frozen vegetables, Canned vegetables

## Beverages

**Definition:** Drinks and drink mixes.

**Examples:** Soda, Protein Shakes, Coffee, Tea, Juice

## Household Items

**Definition:** Products not intended as recipe ingredients or meal-planning foods.

**Examples:** Vitamins, Supplements, Non-food household products

This category should remain small.

## When in Doubt

Place products according to how a shopper would search for them.

If an item could reasonably fit two categories, choose the category where the average
household would expect to find it — not the category that matches its ingredients.

Examples:
- Beef Jerky → Snacks (not Meat & Poultry)
- Frozen Chicken Nuggets → Frozen (not Meat & Poultry)
- Rotisserie Chicken → Meat & Poultry (a core protein, not a snack)
- Ranch Seasoning Packet → Seasonings & Spices (not Condiments & Sauces — it's a dry mix,
  not a ready-to-use sauce)
- BBQ Sauce → Condiments & Sauces (not Seasonings & Spices — it's ready to pour on, not a
  spice blend)

This rule applies to every category, including ones without an explicit definition
above — when uncertain, default to shopper-intuition over ingredient composition or
keyword matching.

---

*This document is the persistent source of truth for ingredient categorization
philosophy. Consult it before applying keyword-based category rules — these
shopping-behavior rules take priority when they conflict with what a simple keyword
match would suggest (e.g. "Beef Jerky" contains "beef" but belongs in Snacks, not
Meat & Poultry).*
